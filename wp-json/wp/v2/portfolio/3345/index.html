OVERVIEW<\/span><\/p>\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t

\n\t\t\t\t
\n\t\t\t

\nBritish Columbia is a home to sixth largest park system in the world, and also to highest diversity of life in Canada. BC Park Foundation was created to reduce the risks imposed on the environment by pollution, and also to enhance the parks services for better preservation and enjoyment of our beautiful nature.\n<\/span><\/p>\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t

\n\t\t\t\t
\n\t\t\t

\nFor this project, BC Parks has requested students at Emily Carr University to design effective means for the foundation to promote themselves to the public and get the support they need in terms of finance or physical help. \n<\/span><\/p>\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t

APPROACH<\/span><\/p>\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t

\n\t\t\t\t
\n\t\t\t

\nWe pitched the idea of an interactive phone booth that informs the public of the roles BC Parks Foundation is playing in protecting the nature. Following that information is a page that asks for the public's involvement in the organization through financial means.\n<\/span><\/p>\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t

\n\t\t\t\t

\nHowever, if the booth users decline to donate, the screen automatically transitions to a page where it talks about respectful etiquette when visiting parks.
